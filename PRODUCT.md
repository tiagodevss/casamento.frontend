# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Wedding guests of Tiago & Gabriela (family, friends, church community), visiting on mobile and desktop to learn event details, read the couple's story, RSVP, and contribute gifts. Many guests are older relatives, so clarity and legibility matter alongside the romantic tone. Visitors arrive once or twice (before the wedding, maybe to check details again), not repeat users.

## Product Purpose

A single-page wedding invitation site for Tiago & Gabriela's wedding on November 14, 2026, at Igreja Universal Paulínia. It tells the couple's story (chapters from first meeting to proposal), shows event details and countdown, collects RSVPs, and offers a gift/contribution list. Success = guests feel the significance of the day, know exactly when/where to be, and RSVP or contribute easily.

## Brand Personality

Elegant and formal, with a romantic, storybook thread (lanterns, night sky, chapters) already established in the codebase. Voice: warm but composed — this is a formal church wedding, not a casual party. Emotional goal: guests should feel the occasion is significant, tender, and beautifully considered, without tipping into twee or overly playful.

## Anti-references

None specified. No accessibility or stylistic sites to explicitly avoid.

## Design Principles

- Formality over whimsy when the two pull in different directions — the lantern/storybook motifs are decorative accents, not the dominant tone.
- Legibility for all ages — guests span generations; body text and RSVP/gift interactions must stay comfortably readable.
- One coherent story arc — the site reads like a single evening unfolding (hero → story → gallery → details → RSVP → gifts → messages), not a set of disconnected sections.
- Preserve the established visual identity (lilac/candle-gold/rose/parchment palette, Cinzel/Cormorant Garamond/Jost type) rather than reinventing it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard accessibility: sufficient contrast for older readers, comfortable touch targets for mobile RSVP/gift forms, no reliance on motion alone to convey information. No specific WCAG level or additional accommodations requested.
